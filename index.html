Chapter I — Origins

The History of
Port Gellhorn

Port Gellhorn did not grow — it was built. In the sweltering summer of 1898, industrialist Charles Gellhorn broke ground on what he called "the gateway to the new Americas." Backed by railroad money and the kind of ambition that the era rewarded handsomely, he dredged the coast, paved the waterfront, and erected the chandleries and counting houses that would make the port one of the most active shipping corridors on the continent.

By the 1930s, the port had evolved beyond cargo. Jazz clubs lined the Esplanade. The Art Deco architecture of downtown — the spires of the Gellhorn Trade Building, the sweeping arches of the Coastal Station — reflected a city that wanted to be taken seriously. It wanted glamour. And for a time, it had it.

The decades that followed brought the familiar cycles of American coastal cities: boom, neglect, reinvention. The shipping lanes shifted north. The jazz clubs became parking lots. But Port Gellhorn's bones were good — and by the early 2000s, a new generation of developers and dreamers had found their way back to the waterfront.

Landmark
The Gellhorn Trade Building

The city's tallest Art Deco structure, completed in 1934 by architect Eloise Morrow. Its brass-fitted lobby is said to be one of the finest surviving examples of the period in the Americas.

History Note
The 1962 Waterfront Riots

A three-day labor dispute that shuttered the port and permanently altered the city's political landscape. The events are still debated in local history circles.

Gellhorn International Raceway

Built for
Speed.

Rumors of the Gellhorn International Raceway have circulated since the first trailer. A sweeping coastal circuit — glimpsed in background signage and a single aerial shot — has become one of the most discussed potential locations in the game.

Community analysis suggests a permanent racing facility on the southern peninsula, with grandstands visible from the coastal highway. Whether it serves as a story mission location, a side activity venue, or simply set dressing remains unconfirmed.

What is certain: the Raceway has become a focal point of Port Gellhorn's identity in the fan community. This site will be your home for Raceway lore, event speculation, and — once the RP portal launches — community race leagues.
